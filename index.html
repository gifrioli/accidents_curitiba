DON'T CHALLENGE BUSES IN CURITIBA

By Giovana Frioli

If you’re not familiar with Curitiba, it is the capital of one of the states in Brazil. The city has more than 2 million inhabitants and has a unique transportation system called “canaletas.” These are exclusive streets where bi-articulated buses run, stopping at small stations distributed throughout the city.

The public transportation system was developed in the 1970s by Jaime Lerner – an architect and former mayor. Recently, the design of these vehicles was an inspiration for the film "Megalopolis", directed by Francis Coppola.

Despite the good story, Curitiba has a problem with cyclists, pedestrians, and public buses. Whether due to competitions on the bus lines among teenagers or people crossing the street inattentively, the city is one of the capitals where the number of such accidents is growing the most in Brazil.

Data from Urbanização de Curitiba (URBS), responsible for public transportation in the capital of Paraná, show the number of accidents involving buses, cyclists and pedestrians in the city. The information below was obtained through Brazil’s Access to Information Law.

There have been 268 accidents involving buses and cyclists in Curitiba, in the last five years.

The red dots represent the deaths and the locations where the accidents occurred.

Buses have run over 257 pedestrians in the last five years.

During this period, 11 people died due to buses in the city.

Most pedestrian and cyclist run-overs in Curitiba occur on the city’s exclusive bus lanes. The graph below shows that the accidents happen mainly during peak hours, when people are going to or coming back from work. Currently, more than one million citizens use public transportation daily.

DANGEROUS CHALLENGE BEHIND BUSES

There is a challenge among young cyclists and teenagers called "rabeira." It involves holding onto the back of buses to gain speed on the exclusive bus lanes. There are several reports in the professional media about deaths or injuries caused by this challenge.

No data is available regarding the number of accidents involving this practice. However, in April, a 15-year-old teenager died after doing the "rabeira" behind a public bus. Young people share videos on social media showing the adrenaline rush of taking on the challenge.
